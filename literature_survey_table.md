# LITERATURE SURVEY

| YEAR | PAPER TITLE with author | METHODOLOGY | ADVANTAGES & DISADVANTAGES | LIMITATIONS | FUTURE SCOPE |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 2025 | Digital Escrow and Conditional Payment Logic Using Blockchain for B2B Cross-Border Trade - Vikas Reddy Mandadhi | Proposed framework for blockchain-based digital escrow in cross-border B2B trade using smart contracts, oracle verification, permissioned ledgers, and compliance modules. | **Adv:** Automates conditional settlement; reduces latency and intermediary dependence; improves transparency, auditability, and fraud resistance. **Disadv:** Depends on reliable oracle inputs; faces interoperability issues across jurisdictions and platforms; adoption may be institutionally slow. | Framework-oriented study without implementation benchmarking; limited mainly to cross-border B2B trade scenarios; regulatory harmonization and digital identity integration remain unresolved. | AI-driven risk scoring; interoperable trade networks; tokenized trade assets; programmable trade documents; decentralized dispute-resolution mechanisms. |
| 2024 | Escrow Mechanisms for Tokenization of Real World Assets (RWA) \| Digital Trust for Physical Wealth - Sercan Koc | Conceptual review of RWA tokenization and escrow mechanisms, covering applications, theoretical perspectives, and integration challenges across multiple asset classes. | **Adv:** Increases liquidity through fractionalization; broadens investor access; reduces intermediary costs; improves transparency and security. **Disadv:** Legal enforceability is uncertain; smart-contract and oracle risks remain; user complexity may hinder adoption. | Largely conceptual rather than empirically validated; broad scope limits asset-specific depth; depends on evolving regulatory and technical ecosystems. | Cross-chain interoperability standards; advanced cryptography and formal verification; AI/ML for fraud and risk detection; stronger decentralized oracles; regulatory development. |
| 2025 | Blockchain Signatures to Ensure Information Integrity and Non-Repudiation in the Digital Era: A comprehensive study - Kaveri Banerjee, Sajal Saha | Comparative cryptographic survey of blockchain signature schemes based on non-repudiation, integrity, efficiency, scalability, compatibility, and application suitability. | **Adv:** Offers structured comparison of signature families; clarifies security-efficiency trade-offs; identifies ECDSA as a strong balance of security and performance. **Disadv:** Conventional RSA/DSA become inefficient at higher key sizes; privacy-preserving schemes may increase storage or verification cost; IoT settings remain challenging. | Literature-based analysis with limited deployment evidence; newer blockchain-optimized signatures are still underexplored; interactions with consensus, privacy, and storage are not fully resolved. | Improve anonymity with ring and blind signatures; combine signatures with identity authentication and timestamping; design aggregate signatures for IoT; develop efficient lattice-based and aggregate schemes. |
| 2025 | AI Agents Meet Blockchain: A Survey on Secure and Scalable Collaboration for Multi-Agents - Md Monjurul Karim, Dong Hoang Van, Sangeen Khan, Qiang Qu, Yaroslav Kholodov | Systematic literature survey using keywords, inclusion/exclusion criteria, and multi-database screening to study AI-agent and blockchain integration. | **Adv:** Gives a comprehensive synthesis of AI-blockchain synergy; categorizes practical applications; clearly identifies major research challenges and trends. **Disadv:** Integration still faces computational overhead, transaction latency, storage growth, privacy risks, and cross-chain complexity. | Based on a still-emerging evidence base; excludes primary experimental benchmarking; inclusion of preprints means evidence maturity varies across studies. | Privacy-preserving multi-agent collaboration; cross-chain protocols and standard APIs; fully on-chain AI and tokenized AI agents; explainability tools; governance for self-sovereign agents; decentralized quantum and regulatory research. |
